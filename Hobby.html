My Hobbies

Running

I developed a passion for running back in middle school. Ironically, I used to hate it — it felt like a painful task, and I was always among the slowest in class. But things changed when I had to prepare for our physical fitness exam. To improve my performance, I started running every day. Gradually, through this daily routine, I discovered that I was no longer forcing myself — I had actually fallen in love with running. Eventually, I began to achieve high scores in the tests. However, over time, my focus shifted from performance to the experience itself. Running became a form of self-dialogue, a moment of complete relaxation where I could focus solely on my breathing and listen to my inner voice. It taught me the value of perseverance — a quality I now carry into my academic work and everyday life.

When faced with difficulties, I’ve learned how to persist through them, one step at a time. Later, against my parents’ wishes, I signed up for a 10km marathon. Each race pushed me beyond my limits and gave me a strong sense of accomplishment. For me, the result isn’t what matters — it's the journey, the effort, and the resilience along the way. I hope to challenge myself with 20km or even 40km marathons in the future, just as I strive to gradually move forward in life and climb higher, one goal at a time.

Drawing

Although I come from an art background, I wouldn’t call myself a “qualified” art student. While others can sketch effortlessly with graceful, fluid lines, my drawings often resemble childish doodles. At first, I quietly vented my frustrations on Xiaohongshu, upset about my limitations. But somehow, I started recording things from my daily life through drawing — and unexpectedly, many people resonated with my work. That sense of connection was a pleasant surprise! (Although the past two years have been hectic and slowed me down, I’m determined to keep drawing in the future.) 

Many of my works may have been inspired by Diary of a Wimpy Kid — one of my childhood favorites! But honestly, I don’t mind. I simply want to use my stick-figure-like characters to capture moments that feel meaningful to me. After all, while I may not be good at drawing, I do know how to tell stories. And sharing my own stories, in my own voice, is something I take pride in — and will continue to pursue.

Travelling

If you were to ask me, “Which is your favorite activity?”  I would say “Traveling!”

Thanks to my parents' influence, I’ve been drawn to the charm of travel since I was a child. Our family makes an effort to go on a trip together every year, exploring different parts of the world. So far, we’ve visited many countries, including New Zealand, Italy, Norway, Sweden, Denmark, Thailand, and Indonesia, among others. As my dad always says, “The more you see, the more you realize that most things in life — people or problems — are really no big deal.”

Later on, I started to enjoy traveling with my friends — or even on my own. We always look for something a bit more adventurous. For example, we once went on a secret road trip through northwest China without telling our parents. Sometimes we go hiking in nature (my absolute favorite — there’s nothing better than being surrounded by nature!), or even skinny-dipping on deserted beaches.

Volunteer Trip

During the summer after my college graduation, I traveled alone to Weizhou Island to work as a volunteer. Weizhou Island is a remote island located in Guangxi, China. Since everything on the island has to be transported manually, the cost of living there is higher than in other parts of Guangxi. I stayed for about half a month, helping the hotel cleaners during the day by tidying rooms and changing bed sheets. In the evening, once the weather cooled down, I was free to explore the island. While there, I met many people, and we treated each other with genuine kindness. I was deeply touched by their simple and sincere way of life—especially my boss, who was an amazing cook.Thanks to him, I definitely got my fill of seafood! The raw beauty and natural rhythm of island life left a lasting impression on me.
