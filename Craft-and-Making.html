Early Design Exploration

HOBNOB

As wine and tea culture continues to evolve and spread, it has attracted growing interest from people around the world. Many enthusiasts not only collect fine wines and teas, but also create a rising demand for functional and elegant storage solutions for related vessels. 

The HOBNOB design features a rotating structure that allows easy access to utensils. Its internal compartments offer secure storage for fragile cups, protecting them from damage. Integrating the form of a tea table, the piece combines display and practicality, catering to the social needs of younger users who value convenience and ritual in everyday gatherings.

Bird's Nest Lamp

The Bird’s Nest Lamp is my very first handcrafted product — an ambient lamp designed to help weary office workers unwind after a long day. 

Inspired by the form of a bird’s nest, the lamp aims to reconnect people with nature. It invites users to slow down, notice the natural world around them, and reflect on the idea that nature is not only a source of comfort, but ultimately, our true home.

 Phoenix

Phoenix is an automata sculpture inspired by the legendary Chinese phoenix — a timeless symbol of rebirth and grace.

The internal structure is assembled using traditional mortise-and-tenon joinery, a time-honored woodworking technique that requires no nails or screws.By turning the side handle, the wooden mechanism is set in motion: the body gently bobs up and down while the wings open and close in rhythmic harmony, mimicking the majestic motion of flight. 

Crafted from American white oak, the piece embraces a natural and unrefined texture, reflecting both simplicity and strength — qualities often associated with the mythical bird itself.

Doris

Doris is a sustainable cosmetic packaging project developed in collaboration with Eastman. The outer shell is made from Eastman’s biodegradable materials, significantly reducing environmental impact.
 
A key feature lies in its refillable inner cartridge design — after use, the user can simply replace the inner unit while continuing to reuse the original shell, promoting long-term sustainability. The inner container, made of silicone and polyamide, is durable, easy to press, and maintains its shape. It can also be refilled for multiple uses.

Compact and travel-friendly, Doris is designed for users frequently on the go. Its compressible structure makes it easy to carry without deformation, combining sustainability with convenience.

Hidden Forest

This is my second lighting design. Unlike the previous static piece, this lamp incorporates a rotatable interactive mechanism, offering users a more engaging and playful experience. 

Inspired by the traditional Chinese "Zou Ma Deng" (rotating lantern) and the poetic imagery of "a small bridge over flowing water", the lamp showcases miniature village scenes and pandas, modeled in Rhino and fabricated via 3D printing. 

When gently pushed, the lamp begins to spin, and light projects through the hollowed patterns, casting dynamic shadows on the wall — revealing a "hidden forest" that blends traditional aesthetics with modern fabrication.

Double Sided Clock

Some students find studying boring and lack self-discipline. Moreover, timer apps on mobile phones can often become a source of distraction rather than focus. In response to this issue, this design aims to make clocks more engaging while also introducing a slight sense of pressure, in order to help students improve their study efficiency.

The alarm clock features a double-sided design, allowing users to see the time from both the front and the back. Its hour and minute hands move counterclockwise, subtly creating a sense of urgency to encourage focus. On the front side, a frosted panel obscures all but the key time markers, directing the student's attention toward specific study intervals and reducing unnecessary distractions.

Her Side Effects

In today’s society, women often face anxiety caused by social expectations — about appearance, weight, and safety. Due to biological differences, they are also more likely to experience physical and emotional pain. This bracelet series reflects some of these struggles.

 The first piece is made using ibuprofen and birth control pills, mixed with red pigment to symbolize pain and blood, highlighting issues like period cramps and fear of pregnancy.

 The second piece includes handmade clay desserts such as cake and chocolate, combined with diet pills, representing the conflict between binge eating and the pressure to stay thin. This work aims to bring attention to the hidden challenges women face — both physically and mentally.

Because this project captures the pain points of today’s women, it has become a hit on Xiaohongshu! The introduction post received over 10,000 likes!

Whispers of the Sea

During my time as a volunteer on Weizhou Island in Guangxi, I often saw large amounts of plastic bottles and household waste scattered along the shore—a sight that deeply saddened me. In stark contrast, colorful and delicate seashells lay quietly nearby. 

In response, I began experimenting with heating and reshaping discarded plastic bottles, combining them with the shells to create handmade jewelry. Through this process, I aim to explore the intersection of regeneration and beauty, while conveying an environmental message: waste does not mark the end of an object’s life—it can be transformed and given new meaning.
