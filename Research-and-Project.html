Current Research & Project

Volkswagen ID.7 – Scenario-Based Digital Service Demo

2024.6-2024.8

This project was developed in collaboration with Volkswagen to create a scenario-based demo for their 2024 ID.7 model.
The demo was designed for iPad-based in-store presentations, allowing sales staff to guide customers through key digital services offered by the vehicle.

 The experience consists of three main modules: 1) Full cabin showcase 2) Charging management 3) Nighttime driving mode.
My contribution focused on the charging management scenario and the overall UI design of the demo.

 I concentrated on interactive environment design, HMI (Human-Machine Interface) flows, and cockpit configuration, ensuring an intuitive and immersive digital interaction experience.

XR & SSDT for Smart Electric Bus Design (with King Long)

2024.9-2024.11

This project was conducted in collaboration with King Long Group, aiming to explore the feasibility of integrating XR and Super System Digital Twins system for future immersive design and user experiences.

 Within the Unity-based prototype, all interactions were categorized into four major components: environment, scenario, product, and digital humans. This systematic approach enabled the construction of complex service and usage scenarios, efficiently simulating multi-dimensional user experiences in regions with limited transportation infrastructure, such as Tibet, China.

CirpDesignPaper

The video below presents an XR-based immersive design validation conducted by Professor Zhang Yan at the Blekinge Institute of Technology (BTH) in Sweden. 

Using a 360° screen, the setup enables participants to experience and evaluate design prototypes in an immersive environment, supporting more effective validation and user-centered assessment.

Paper: Zhang, Y., Larsson, T., Haoqiang, W., & Chunjun, H. (2025). Integrating Digital Twins and Extended Reality for Smart PSS Design: A Case Study on Smart Electric Tour Bus Development. In CIRP DESIGN 2025–35th CIRP Design Conference, 2025 02-04 April 2025, Patras, Greece.

Scenario-Driven Cockpit Design for Electric Vans Serving Future European Logistics

2024.10-2025.2

As manufacturing becomes increasingly digital, smart vehicle design is shifting toward scenario-driven, service-oriented systems to meet diverse needs across different countries. 

In collaboration with Chery, this project explores key scenarios in the European logistics industry. Using virtual simulation technology, we developed a scenario-driven design model for intelligent cockpits. 

Results show that virtual simulation enhances early-stage prototyping and decision-making, enabling cockpit designs that dynamically adapt to the cultural, regulatory, and user needs of various European regions.

HCII2025Paper

The experimental project was developed into a research paper, which I presented at the HCII 2025 conference in Gothenburg, Sweden, on June 25, 2025.

MobiScene: Immersive Sustainable Van Configurator for European City

2025.4-2025.6

This project, developed in collaboration with Chery Automobile, investigates a modular vehicular system tailored to evolving urban lifestyles in Europe. It presents many reconfigurable scenarios—ranging from sustainable beverage services to mobile workspaces . 

Each scenario supports full customization, encompassing exterior form, interior configuration, and virtual interactions. The immersive platform enables multi-perspective exploration within dynamically shifting environmental contexts. Sustainability is embedded throughout the design, utilizing eco-conscious materials and adaptable infrastructures. 

The project envisions the vehicle not merely as transport, but as a flexible, sustainable habitat for future urban mobility.

Visualizing Scenario-Based Optimization through Digital Twins and XR

2025.7-present

This ongoing collaborative project aims to integrate optimization algorithms for electric commercial vehicles with a digital twin environment under the same operational scenario. In collaboration with a PhD student from a management science background, we explore how scenario-based planning in logistics can be better understood and evaluated through XR-assisted visualization. 

My main responsibility is to transform data-driven outputs into concrete, interactive simulations using Unity-based digital twins methods, allowing stakeholders to intuitively assess and compare the effectiveness of different solutions. 

This project seeks to bridge quantitative modeling with human-centered decision-making, and a paper is expected to be produced in the later stages.
